AI fatigue is not burnout. Burnout announces itself. AI fatigue sneaks in — you slowly lose the feeling that you wrote the code, that you solved the problem, that you know what you're doing. This sheet helps you recognize it and do something about it.

"Did I actually figure this out — or did I just accept what the AI told me?"
That question is the first signal. If you can't answer it confidently, you're in the fatigue zone.
4 Tiers of AI Fatigue — Which Are You?
🌿 Tier 1
Holding Up
Score 0–3
Aware of AI fatigue risks. Actively maintaining boundaries. Focused on staying sharp.
🌤 Tier 2
Some Fatigue
Score 4–7
Occasional doubt about your own contributions. Occasionally delegating decisions to AI.
🌧 Tier 3
Real Fatigue
Score 8–11
Regular ghost authorship. Skill confidence eroding. Starting to avoid problems you used to enjoy.
🌑 Tier 4
Need a Break
Score 12–15
Significant skill erosion. Crisis-level doubt. Consider structured reset + professional support.
5 Core Recovery Tactics
01
The Explanation Requirement
Before accepting any AI suggestion: explain it out loud. If you can't, you don't own it yet.
02
No-AI Focus Blocks
2–4 hours per week with zero AI. Write code. Solve problems. Feel the struggle. That's the point.
03
Skill Audit
List 5 things you could do without AI 2 years ago. Rate your current confidence 1–5. Pick 1 to rebuild.
04
Calibration Log
Track where AI was right vs. where you were right. Over time you'll see your calibration drift.
05
Sunday No-AI Block
One morning a week. Coffee. Code. No suggestions. No autocomplete. Just you and the problem.

What AI Can't Take
Contextual judgment. Knowing why something matters, not just how. Trust-based influence. Reading a room, building consensus. Ethical calibration. Deciding what shouldn't be built. Taste. Knowing what good looks like. Institutional narrative. Why this system exists here.
Key Distinction
Imposter syndrome = cognitive distortion. You know more than you think.

AI fatigue = functional change. Your skills have genuinely shifted — partially due to AI use. Both can be true at once. The recovery paths are different.
When to Get Help
If you're experiencing: hopelessness, physical symptoms (chest tightness, panic attacks, insomnia), extended inability to work, or thoughts of self-harm — reach out.
