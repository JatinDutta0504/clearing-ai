The 8 Principles of Recovery

1
Start before you're ready. Recovery doesn't require motivation. It requires a decision.
2
Small actions compound. A 10-minute no-AI coding session is not nothing. It's a foundation.
3
Feeling worse before better is normal. Naming the problem often makes it feel bigger before it gets better.
4
Skill rebuild is slower than expected. The first no-AI debug session will feel slow. That's the point.
5
Rest is not the same as recovery. Passive rest helps. Active reflection accelerates.
6
Community is a multiplier. Telling one honest person what you're going through changes the math.
7
AI is not the enemy. The relationship with AI is the problem. That relationship can be rebuilt.
8
This is not linear. Some weeks you'll feel back to normal. Some days you'll feel worse than Day 1. Both are fine.

After Day 30 Your Recovery Continues

Signs you're recovering from AI fatigue:

  • You can sit with a hard problem without immediately reaching for AI
  • You notice yourself learning something without AI's help and feeling good about it
  • Sunday night dread has lessened (not disappeared — that's not realistic)
  • You can explain the code you shipped this week in detail
  • You're curious about something in your work again
  • You set and kept at least one AI boundary this month
  • You told someone honest truth about how you're doing
