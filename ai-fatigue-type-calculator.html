        All Five AI Fatigue Types

        Most engineers have elements of several types. One usually dominates. Here's the full picture:

        🎯 Skill Erosion You know less than you did. AI has bypassed your learning loop. Recovery requires deliberate unassisted practice.
        ⚖️ Decision Fatigue AI creates hundreds of micro-decisions. Your decision budget is exhausted before noon. Recovery requires batching and boundaries.
        🪪 Identity Erosion You're not sure who you are as an engineer anymore. The work gets done but doesn't feel like yours. Recovery requires ownership signals.
        🔀 Attention Fragmentation You can't focus. Every AI ping resets your cognitive state. The 23-minute recovery window never completes. Recovery requires focus architecture.
        🔄 Workflow Displacement Your natural problem-solving process has been replaced by AI prompt loops. You can't sit with a problem anymore. Recovery requires rebuilding the process.

        Why does this distinction matter?

        Treating all AI fatigue the same leads to generic advice that doesn't land. "Take breaks" helps attention fatigue but not decision fatigue. "Use AI less" helps skill erosion but doesn't address identity loss. Knowing your type lets you target the actual mechanism — which is why recovery actually works faster.
