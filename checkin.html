The research

Why one question a day actually helps

Not fluff. Cognitive science.

🧠

Interrupts automatic processing

Kahneman's System 1 runs on autopilot. A single honest question activates System 2 thinking — the reflective, deliberate kind. Even 30 seconds of it compounds over months.

📍

Identity anchoring

Research on occupational identity shows that brief daily reflection helps maintain a stable sense of self during periods of rapid role disruption — exactly what AI-era engineering creates.

🌱

Habit stacking

BJ Fogg's work shows tiny habits — ones that take under 2 minutes — are sustainable. A one-question daily ritual is small enough to survive even the most intense sprint cycles.

📝

Expressive writing benefits

Even brief expressive writing (Pennebaker, 1997) reduces cognitive load, improves mood, and helps process work-related stress. You don't need a full journal — a sentence or two is enough.

    About the daily check-in

    Each day you get a single honest question about your relationship with engineering, AI tools, and your own craft. Write a short reflection (or just sit with it), hit save, and you're done. Your answers live only in your browser. Nothing leaves your device. Your streak tracks how many consecutive days you've checked in.
    Everything is stored in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server — there is no server. No account, no email, no tracking. Your reflections are completely private. They exist only on your device. If you clear your browser data, they'll be gone — so consider exporting occasionally if they matter to you.
    Because you're already overwhelmed. The whole premise of this site is that you don't need more — you need a moment to actually think. One question takes 30 seconds. That's the dose. Enough to interrupt the spiral. Enough to bring you back. And if you want more, there's the private journal and the 30-day practice.
    Your streak resets to zero. But here's what matters: the reflection you wrote the day before still happened. The practice still helped. Streaks are motivation tools, not moral scores. Miss a day, start again. No one is watching. No judgment here. Come back when you can.
    Engineers who've sat with AI fatigue themselves. These aren't generic wellness prompts. They're aimed specifically at the disorientation of being a software engineer in an AI-saturated environment — the loss of craft ownership, the erosion of deep thinking, the quiet identity crisis. They're written to land where it actually hurts.
    Yes. There's a small reset link below the streak display. It will clear all your saved answers and reset your streak to zero. This can't be undone. Sometimes a clean start is exactly what you need.
