Daily Practice

Small rituals that help engineers come back to themselves.

Morning (5 minutes)

Before opening any IDE or chat window, write one sentence: What do I actually want to accomplish today? Not what's in the backlog. What you, as a person, want to do. Even if the answer is "get through the day."

During Work

Once a day, solve one small problem without AI assistance. It doesn't have to be important. It can be writing a simple function, debugging a small issue, or reading documentation directly. The goal is to remind yourself that you still can.

Take at least one 15-minute break where you are not reading or looking at screens. Walk. Sit outside. Let your brain idle.

Evening (5 minutes)

Write one thing you understood today that you didn't understand yesterday. One sentence is enough. This practice, compounded over months, becomes a record of your growth as an engineer — growth that exists entirely in you, not in any model.

Weekly

Review your private journal entries from the week. Notice patterns. Are you more anxious on certain days? Do some types of work leave you energized while others drain you? Use this data about yourself the way you'd use metrics — to make better decisions.
