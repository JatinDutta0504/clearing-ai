What Kind of Fatigued Are You?

AI fatigue shows up differently depending on who you are. Four patterns we see.

The Skeptic-Adopter

You were never a true believer, but you adopted anyway.

You still use the tools because not using them feels like professional suicide. But every time AI gets something subtly wrong and nobody catches it, you feel a quiet dread — because you know you're the last person paying attention. You're tired of being right about things nobody wants to hear.

What helps: Find one peer who shares your skepticism. That conversation alone can dissolve weeks of isolation.

The True Believer

You were early. You were enthusiastic. Now you're not sure what happened.

You shipped more with AI. You loved it. But somewhere along the way, the work stopped feeling like yours. You can't remember the last time you solved something hard and felt proud of how you did it — only that you got it done. The output is fine. The feeling is hollow.

What helps: One small project, no AI, no deadline. Just you and the problem. Remind yourself you still know how to build things.

The Overwhelmed Senior

Years of experience. Now you're reviewing code faster than you can actually read it.

You're the human in the loop for the whole team. Everyone trusts you to catch what AI misses — and you do, most of the time. But you're exhausted. The pace has doubled and your judgment is the only thing standing between working code and production incidents. Nobody talks about the cognitive cost of that.

What helps: Slow reviews down. Make it a team norm to review AI code more carefully, not faster. You are not a bottleneck. You are quality.

The Junior Who Skipped Ahead

You learned to code in the AI era. Now you're not sure what you actually know.

You ship. You fix bugs. But sometimes, in quiet moments, you wonder if you'd be able to do any of this without AI. You never really had to. The foundation feels shaky in a way you can't articulate. You're afraid to find out if your instincts are real.

What helps: Go back to basics on purpose. Build something small from scratch, with documentation only. You'll surprise yourself.
