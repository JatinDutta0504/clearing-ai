Engagement Responses (Prepare These)

If someone says "another burnout site":

Fair skepticism. What makes this different: it focuses specifically on the AI fatigue phenomenon — the gap between looking productive and actually being engaged. It's not about meditation apps. It's about the cognitive and identity mechanisms that AI use disrupts in engineers specifically.

If someone asks about imposter syndrome vs AI fatigue:

Good distinction. AI fatigue means your internal narrative is actually accurate — you don't own the code because you didn't develop it. Imposter syndrome is a cognitive distortion where your narrative doesn't match reality. The site has a full comparison page with a self-assessment that helps you figure out which one you're dealing with.

If someone asks about skill atrophy:

Documented in Bainbridge (1983) "Ironies of Automation" and Parasuraman's automation bias research. The mechanism: productive struggle is where skill gets encoded. When AI bypasses the struggle, the skill doesn't form — even if the correct code appears. It's reversible, but it requires deliberate practice with AI off.

If someone says "just take breaks":

Rest helps burnout. It doesn't fully address AI fatigue, which has a cognitive dimension — the gap between what you understand and what you've shipped. The site has a full breakdown of why they're different and what each requires.

If someone asks about tracking/data:

Zero tracking, zero analytics, zero cookies. Everything runs in your browser's localStorage. No data leaves your machine.
