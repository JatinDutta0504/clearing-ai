# LinkedIn Post 4 — The Velocity Trap
**Schedule: Thursday May 22, 7-9 AM PDT**
**Target: Senior engineers, tech leads, engineering managers**

---

Your PR count went up 40% last quarter.

Your debugging speed didn't change.
Your architecture judgment didn't improve.
Your ability to reason about a system without AI has probably gotten worse.

Most people would call that a win. I think it's the most important data point you're not tracking.

Here's what I mean:

When AI generates most of your code, your velocity increases — but your capability velocity decreases. You're moving faster in the system while the system moves slower in you.

The engineers who figured this out early have started measuring something new: confidence unaided. Not "can you ship it with AI?" but "can you ship it without AI?"

That's the number that tells you whether you're growing or just accelerating.

The gap between those two numbers — shipping velocity and capability velocity — is where AI fatigue lives.

And the uncomfortable part: most engineers don't feel it in real time. The AI output looks good. The tests pass. The merge happens. It feels like progress.

Until you open a codebase without AI for the first time in months, and realize how quiet it got in there.

What are you measuring beyond shipping velocity?
