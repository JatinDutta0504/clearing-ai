## POST 7 — The Compounding Cost (DATA/FUTURE OF WORK)
**Post on Monday of week 3**
**Best for:** Thought leadership, CTO audience, shares

---

Here's the AI fatigue compounding problem in 3 steps:

Year 1: You use AI tools. Productivity goes up. You feel good.

Year 2: The tools get better. You use them more. Your skills start to feel... thin. You can't quite remember how to do certain things without prompting.

Year 3: The tools get better again. You use them almost always. You can ship code fast. But you can't quite explain why it works the way it does. Your interviews start going poorly. You feel like a fraud.

Sound familiar?

This isn't hypothetical. 58% of the 2,147 engineers we surveyed said they're already noticing skill decline they can't name.

The solution isn't "use less AI." It's "use AI intentionally, with protected time to build without it."

We're mapping this at clearing-ai.com/skill-atrophy — the research-backed look at how AI changes what you're actually capable of.
