Here's a stat I keep coming back to:

71% of software engineers say they feel like "middlemen" — reviewing AI code rather than writing it.

Not because they're bad at their jobs.

Because the job changed underneath them.

Last quarter, 44% of engineers considered leaving the industry.

Not because they lost passion.

Because the thing they were passionate about — the writing, the problem-solving, the craft — started disappearing.

This is what I keep hearing from engineering managers:

"I don't understand why my best people seem disengaged."

Here's the uncomfortable truth: your best engineers are the ones feeling it hardest.

They care most about craft. They built their identity around building things. They set the highest standards.

AI didn't take their job. It took their satisfaction.

The thing is — this isn't a personal failing. It's a structural problem.

AI fatigue is real. It has a name, a science, and recovery paths.

The resource I built for exactly this: clearing-ai.com/team-manager-guide — conversation scripts, team AI agreement templates, and a practical roadmap for managers navigating this with their teams.
