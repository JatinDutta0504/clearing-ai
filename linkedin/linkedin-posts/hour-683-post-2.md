There's a version of me — the one from two years ago — that would have found this funny.

The 11pm engineer.

It's 11pm. You're off duty. You're watching something mindless. You should be resting.

But your brain won't stop running through the architecture decision from this afternoon. Or the code review that felt off. Or the thing the AI wrote that you're not sure about, and you can't quite figure out why.

You didn't decide to keep working. Your brain never really clocked out.

I started The Clearing because I watched three friends — all senior engineers, all high performers — hit the same wall in the same six months.

They weren't burned out in the old sense. They were doing fine at work.

They were exhausted in a new way. The kind where you ship features and feel nothing. Where you can't quite rest because the work is always still running in the background.

The specific thing that helped them most was the simplest intervention: naming it.

AI fatigue is real. It's different from burnout. And if any of this sounds familiar — you don't have to figure it out alone.

The quiz at clearing-ai.com takes two minutes. It's the honest read I wish I'd had a year ago.

Has your brain ever done the 11pm thing?

👇 I'd genuinely like to know if this is as common as it feels.
