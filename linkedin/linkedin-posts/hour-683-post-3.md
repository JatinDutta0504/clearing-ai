# LinkedIn Post 3 — "The Hidden Costs of Mandatory AI Adoption"
# Hour 683 — Deploy Wednesday May 6, 7-9am local time

---

Your engineering org just mandated AI tool adoption.

Management's framing: "This will make everyone 30% more productive."

That's probably true.

Here's what's probably also true — and nobody's measuring:

**1. Skill erosion at the edges.**
The engineers who used to write the tricky edge cases are now approving AI output they wouldn't have written themselves. The understanding lives with the tool, not the engineer.

**2. Identity displacement.**
Senior engineers built their identity around knowing things. When AI knows more, the ground shifts under the identity — quietly, without announcement.

**3. Attention fragmentation.**
AI-assisted workflows average 40-80 tool interactions per day. Each one is a micro-decision: accept, reject, modify. That's finite cognitive capacity being spent at scale.

**4. Velocity theater.**
The teams shipping faster with AI are often shipping features without understanding the system underneath. The metrics look good. The knowledge is quietly degrading.

**5. Decision fatigue compounding.**
Engineers who used to solve problems now evaluate AI solutions to problems. That's still work — and it's a different kind of work with a different fatigue profile.

The CTOs and engineering directors I talk to who are navigating this well aren't banning AI.

They're protecting the things that would otherwise erode: deep work blocks, no-AI sessions, understanding metrics alongside velocity metrics.

The data on what's actually happening: clearing-ai.com/corporate-ai-wellness

How is your org thinking about this?
