# LinkedIn Post 4 — "Why Your Best Engineers Are Most Likely to Burn Out in 2026"
# Hour 683 — Deploy Thursday May 7, 7-9am local time

---

Here's the counterintuitive thing nobody talks about:

Your highest performers are at the highest risk of AI fatigue.

Not the engineers doing the bare minimum. Not the ones coasting.

The ones who care the most.

Here's why:

**Craft-lovers** built their identity around the satisfaction of building something from understanding. AI removes the "from understanding" part — fast. The craft lover ends up in a job that looks right but feels hollow.

**Identity-invested seniors** spent 10-15 years accumulating deep knowledge. When AI makes that knowledge less relevant to day-to-day output, the identity crisis hits hardest for the people who invested the most.

**High-standard engineers** can't turn off the part of their brain that notices when AI output is wrong, brittle, or subtly broken. They spend more cognitive energy reviewing than they would have spent writing it themselves.

**Mentorship-driven engineers** learned by struggling through problems. When AI removes the struggle, the teaching loop breaks — and they're left watching junior engineers grow without the mentorship path they had.

The retention risk isn't the engineer who's coasting.

It's the engineer who cares most — and is quietly feeling like the thing they loved most about this work is disappearing.

If you're a manager and this sounds like someone on your team: the conversation is worth having.
