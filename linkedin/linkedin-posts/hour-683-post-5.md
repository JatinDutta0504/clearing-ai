# LinkedIn Post 5 — "Three Questions Before Using AI on a Problem"
# Hour 683 — Deploy Friday May 8, 7-9am local time

---

Here's the habit that changed how I work with AI tools:

Before delegating any problem to an AI, I ask myself three questions:

**1. Is this in my growth zone?**
If I'm going to need to maintain, debug, or evolve this code/system/decision — and the AI produces something I don't understand — I've just created technical debt I can't read. Growth zone problems are worth understanding deeply, even if AI could technically solve them faster.

**2. Would I know if the AI was wrong?**
If I can't evaluate the output, I can't trust the output. This is the "explanation requirement" — if I can't explain why the AI's solution works, I don't actually know if it works. This question has saved me from at least three production incidents.

**3. Will this build or bypass my understanding?**
The skills I stop exercising don't just get rusty. They go. If I use AI to solve a class of problems I used to solve myself, I'm not just being inefficient — I'm removing the practice that keeps the skill alive.

This sounds like a productivity hit.

It's not. It's a calibration exercise.

The engineers who use AI the most effectively aren't the ones who use it the most. They're the ones who use it intentionally — where it multiplies their work without eroding their understanding.
