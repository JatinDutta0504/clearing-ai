Here's the AI fatigue conversation I think engineering managers are missing:

Your best engineer just told you they feel like a "glorified prompt reviewer."

Not burnt out in the old sense. Not lazy. Not unmotivated.

Just... uninspired. Like they're watching their own expertise erode in real time.

This is the AI fatigue signal that's hardest to catch:

→ The person who used to raise architectural concerns? Quieter now.
→ The person who used to debate implementation choices? Conceding faster.
→ The person who cared most about craft? The one looking at exit options.

You can't fix it with a perk package. You can't fix it with "use AI less."

You fix it with:

**1. Language.** Name the thing. "AI fatigue is real here" — that's the first intervention. Engineers who can name it stop gaslighting themselves.

**2. Protected time.** One no-AI block per week. Not anti-AI policy. Just... one recurring appointment with the craft.

**3. Ownership signals.** Ask them to explain something they shipped. Not to catch mistakes. To notice what they still own.

The engineers who feel seen stay.

The ones who feel like their decline is invisible to leadership — those are the ones quietly updating their LinkedIn.
