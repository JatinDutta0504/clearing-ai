A lot of engineers think they have imposter syndrome.

### Tweet 2

```
Imposter Syndrome = cognitive distortion.
You *can* do the work. You just don't feel like you can.

AI Fatigue = functional change.
Your learning loop broke. Your code isn't yours. The problem is real — not in your head.

One is a perception problem. One is a structural problem.
```

---

### Tweet 3

```
Why engineers confuse them:

Both feel like: "I don't deserve this role."
Both feel like: "Someone's going to find out."

But AI Fatigue also comes with:
→ You can't explain code you shipped
→ You stopped learning 3 months ago
→ Sunday nights feel hollow even after good weeks
→ Your skills feel like they're dissolving

Those aren't IS. That's structural.
```

---

### Tweet 4

```
The tell: AI Fatigue responds to *behavior change*.
No-AI mornings. Explanation requirements. Deliberate practice.

Imposter Syndrome responds to *evidence reframing*.
Getting evidence you *can*. Internalizing success.

Same feeling. Different solution. Wrong diagnosis = wrong fix.
```
