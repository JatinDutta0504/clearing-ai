Hey,

Two weeks ago, you took the quiz. Maybe you shared it with someone on your team. Maybe you even opened the 30-day checklist.

And then life happened.

That's normal. This email is for the people who started but didn't finish — and for the new folks who just joined after Hacker News.

Here's what the engineers who are actually recovering have in common:

**They explain AI's code out loud before accepting it.**

Not "review it." Not "think about it." Out loud. To themselves. Like they're teaching someone.

This is called the Explanation Requirement, and it's the most consistently underrated recovery tactic we found in 2,000+ engineer conversations.

Why it works:

When you can't explain a piece of code — when the words don't come — that's your brain telling you: this part is not yours yet. The gap is real. The AI filled a hole you didn't know was there.

When you CAN explain it — when the understanding transfers — that's the learning loop actually closing. The skill stays. The felt competence comes back.

It feels slow. It feels awkward. It feels like you're deliberately making work harder.

That's how you know it's working.

**The 3-question test before accepting any AI code:**
1. Can I explain why this approach was chosen (not just what it does)?
2. Can I name the edge cases it doesn't handle?
3. If I had to rebuild this without AI, where would I start?

If you can't answer all three — the Explanation Requirement just activated.

**Start today:**
→ One code review, one AI suggestion. Explain it out loud before you accept it.
→ No AI for 24 hours after. Let the gap close.
→ Repeat weekly.

This is Week 3 of The Dispatch. If you're on Day 7+ of the 30-day plan — keep going. The hardest part is between Day 10 and 20. That's where the recovery actually starts.

If you haven't started yet: this is your sign.
