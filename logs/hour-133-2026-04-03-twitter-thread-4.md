**Theme:** One day without AI coding tools — why it works and how it changes your relationship with AI

---

**Tweet 1:**
One idea that consistently shows up in our AI Fatigue Quiz data:

Engineers who feel best about their AI use have one thing in common: one day a week, they code without it.

Not productivity advice. It's recalibration.

A thread on why AI Sundays work 👇

---

**Tweet 2:**
The problem isn't using AI.

It's using AI so continuously that you lose the feedback loop.

When you never code without AI, you never get evidence of what you still understand. That evidence — "I built this from scratch and it worked" — is what confidence is actually made of.

Without it, you're running on borrowed certainty.

---

**Tweet 3:**
What an AI Sunday looks like in practice:

→ Pick one small feature or bug
→ Close every AI tab
→ Build or debug from scratch
→ Notice what you reach for vs. what you actually know

You don't have to ship it. You just have to feel the difference between "I can prompt this" and "I can code this."

---

**Tweet 4:**
The data backs it up.

71% of engineers who took our quiz reported that they only took action on AI fatigue after some kind of concrete signal — a failed interview, a moment of panic, something external.

AI Sundays are that signal, but intentional.

You don't wait for the system to fail. You check it yourself.

---

**Tweet 5:**
The engineers I've talked to who do this say the same thing:

After an AI Sunday, Monday feels different. You're not coding FROM AI. You're coding WITH AI — from a place of ownership.

The relationship changes when you know you don't need it.

Try one. Just one.
