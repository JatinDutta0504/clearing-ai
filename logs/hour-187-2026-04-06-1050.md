**Comment:**
> This resonates with the compliance layer nobody talks about. We restricted consumer AI tools after a near-miss where someone pasted a de-identified but re-identifiable dataset into ChatGPT. The ironic part: the engineers building the approved AI workflow tools are the most restricted from using AI in their own work.
>
> There's a specific AI fatigue pattern in healthcare IT that generic wellness advice doesn't address: the three-layer problem. HIPAA compliance adds a cognitive subtext to every AI interaction — "is this prompt safe? Does the service sign a BAA?" Other engineers don't carry that. Clinical decision support systems add another layer: you're building AI that assists in patient care decisions, and you have to maintain calibrated skepticism toward the AI you're building. FDA oversight of SaMD adds documentation overhead on top.
>
> The dual-track coping strategy (AI for boilerplate, manual review for clinical logic) is effective but expensive — that background triage load doesn't show up in any productivity metric.
>
> We built a resource specifically for this: https://clearing-ai.com/ai-healthcare-developer-fatigue.html — it covers the regulatory, cognitive stakes, and tooling constraint layers specifically for healthcare engineers. The advice that works here isn't "use AI less" — it's maintaining access to the domain expertise that makes your AI-assisted work safe.
