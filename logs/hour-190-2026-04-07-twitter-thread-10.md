---

**Tweet 2:**

---

The Tutorial Paradox: the more tutorials you watch, the less confident you feel about your actual skills.

Why?

Because tutorials teach you *about* things. AI tools let you *do* things without learning them.

The gap between "I could explain this" and "I can actually do this" silently widens every time you reach for AI instead of struggling through.

---

**Tweet 3:**

---

The specific damage:

You watch a tutorial on, say, Docker networking.
2 weeks later, AI writes your docker-compose. You accept it.
You feel productive. You also... don't actually know Docker networking.

Repeat this 200 times and you have a senior engineer who can't troubleshoot a basic network issue without AI.

The confidence gap compounds quietly.

---

**Tweet 4:**

---

Here's what nobody tells you:

The solution is NOT to finish all 47 courses before using AI.

It's to be deliberate about which skills you're learning *by struggle* and which you're outsourcing to AI.

The craft skills that got you hired? Protect those.
The tooling that changes every 6 months? Use AI freely.

---

**Tweet 5:**

---

The rule I use:

For every tool I adopt, I do one project the old way first.

Just once. No AI. Just me, the docs, and the frustration.

The frustration IS the learning. Bypassing it once doesn't save time — it costs you the skill.

---

**Tweet 6:**

---

If you recognize this in yourself, you're not behind.

You're just in a field that has not yet figured out how to adopt powerful tools without losing the craft that made the tools worth building.
