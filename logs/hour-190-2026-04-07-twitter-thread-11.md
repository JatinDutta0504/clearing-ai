## Hook: The Sunday Night AI Dread
**When to post:** Fri Apr 10 or Sat Apr 11, 9–11 AM PDT (weekend reflection + Monday prep angle)

---

**Hook Tweet:**

---

Every Sunday night, I open my laptop to "just review the code" before Monday.
I spend 45 minutes staring at AI output I don't remember writing.
I feel vaguely guilty. I don't know what I actually know anymore.

This is called the Sunday Night Reckoning.
If you've felt it: you're not broken.

🧵

---

**Tweet 2:**

---

The Sunday Night Reckoning happens when AI has shipped so much work in your name that you've lost the internal map of your own codebase.

You know what the system does.
You don't know how it does it.
And Sunday night — when there's no one to ask and no AI to bail you out — that's when it hits.

---

**Tweet 3:**

---

The specific feeling:

"I could explain this to someone in a meeting. I could not debug this at 2am."

That's the authorship gap. You shipped the work. You didn't learn it.

AI made you faster. It also made you shallower.

---

**Tweet 4:**

---

Why Sunday specifically?

Because Sunday is when you're not in reactive mode. No standup to prep for. No Slack pings.
Just you and the quiet realization that you don't recognize your own work anymore.

It's not a bad weekend. It's a grief checkpoint.

---

**Tweet 5:**

---

One practice that helps: the Sunday Explanation.

Before Sunday night ends, open one file you worked on this week. Close everything — no AI, no docs, no search.

Write in your own words: what does this do, why is it written this way, what would break if you changed X?

The gap between "I shipped it" and "I understand it" becomes visible immediately.

---

**Tweet 6:**

---

If the Sunday Explanation is painful, that's data.

Not "I'm a bad engineer."
Not "I should work harder."
Just: "I've been delegating my learning to AI and the bill is coming due."

You can start rebuilding any week. It doesn't take a month. It takes one Sunday.
