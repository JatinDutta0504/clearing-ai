### Thread Structure (5 tweets)

**Tweet 1 (Hook):** 
"I've talked to 2000+ engineers in the past month. The pattern is haunting: they're not burned out on work. They're experiencing a slow-motion identity crisis.

Their code shipped. But they don't recognize themselves in it anymore.

**Tweet 2 (The Loss):**
"The specific thing senior engineers feel: watching your craft become invisible. You spent 15 years learning to spot bugs at the pattern level, architect for trade-offs, write code that *teaches*. 

Now you press enter and someone else (an AI) gets credited with exactly those things."

**Tweet 3 (Why It's Different):**
"This isn't imposter syndrome. Imposter syndrome is a cognitive distortion. This is *functional*. Your code ownership literally changed. Your learning loop broken.

And honestly? The industry is gaslighting engineers about this:
• 'Adapt'
• 'Reskill'
• 'Faster velocity means you win'

None of that addresses the grief."

**Tweet 4 (The Insight):**
"Here's what I'm seeing: The engineers adapting healthiest aren't those doubling down on AI tools. They're the ones setting clear boundaries. 

Using AI to *teach* themselves something, not to bypass learning.
Shipping *their* code most days.
Protecting 'no AI' blocks.

The framework is: How do I use this tool without using it to *avoid myself*?"

**Tweet 5 (CTA + Link):**
"If this resonates, I built clearing-ai.com for exactly this.

Free: AI Fatigue Quiz, recovery guides, research, community stories.

Especially for senior ICs and early-career engineers watching their identity slip away.

https://clearing-ai.com"
