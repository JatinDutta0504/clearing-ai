AI FATIGUE RECOVERY CHECKLIST

PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (Days 1-7)
□ Name your fatigue (take the quiz, read diagnosis)
□ Audit your AI usage (tools used daily? % of coding?)
□ Set one boundary (one no-AI block per week)

PHASE 2: REBUILD (Days 8-21)
□ Extend no-AI blocks → 3 hours/week → daily
□ Complete one task without AI assistance

□ Read one "Understand" page (research/science)
□ Share your status with manager/peer

PHASE 3: INTEGRATE (Days 22-28)
□ Define "good AI use" for your role
□ Practice Explanation Requirement (explain AI output)
□ Mentor someone on your recovery
□ Assess: skill recovery? energy shift? identity clarity?
□ Plan next phase (rest? pivot? team change?)

WEEK 4+: SUSTAIN
□ Protect your boundaries (quarterly reset)
□ Monitor for burnout creep (warning signs)
□ Connect with recovery community (clearing-ai.com)
□ Continue learning (deep work, craft focus)
□ Celebrate skill reclamation

RESOURCES:
→ Full recovery guide: clearing-ai.com/recovery
→ Weekly dispatch: clearing-ai.com/newsletter
→ Community stories: clearing-ai.com/stories

### Component 3: "The Dispatch" Weekly Email Template

**Purpose:** Recurring engagement, community building, re-engagement

**Email structure (1 per week):**
1. **Opening hook** (1-2 sentences): Real engineer insight or data point
2. **Essay** (200-300 words): Deep-dive on one AI fatigue topic
3. **Resource**: Link to 1 relevant clearing-ai.com page
4. **Community**: 1 reader quote or story (rotating)
5. **Action**: 1 concrete practice (30-min exercise)
6. **CTA**: "Reply with your story" or "Take the quiz"

**Sample email structure:**
```
Subject: Why your brain can't switch tasks after AI [The Dispatch #1]

Hi [Name],

After you use AI, do you notice it takes forever to focus on something else?

Gloria Mark studied this at UC Irvine. She called it "attention residue." 
On average, it takes engineers 23 minutes 15 seconds to mentally recover 
after a task switch.

AI triggers MORE attention residue than normal interruptions because:
1. You stop mid-problem-solving (the hardest part of thinking)
2. You have to evaluate the AI output (cognitive effort both sides)
3. You're rapidly context-switching between your code and someone else's

→ We wrote the full deep-dive: clearing-ai.com/attention-residue

This week's practice:
Batch your AI usage into ONE 30-minute block instead of scattered throughout 
the day. Track how your focus changes.

What's your experience? Reply with how you notice attention residue in your work.

→ Not seeing the connection to AI fatigue? Take our quiz: clearing-ai.com/quiz

—
The Clearing
Weekly insights on AI fatigue + recovery
clearing-ai.com | Unsubscribe
```
