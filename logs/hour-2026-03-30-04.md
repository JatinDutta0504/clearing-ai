### What 2,000 Engineers Said

Last month, 2,000+ engineers took our AI Fatigue Quiz. We asked them to tell us what they're actually experiencing.

The most common response to "how does AI-assisted coding make you feel?"

**"I thought it was just me."**

That phrase showed up thousands of times.

### This Week's Insight

The cruelest part of AI fatigue for experienced engineers: you're expected to be excited about the productivity gains.

When you're not, you can't even name it without sounding anti-progress. So you stay quiet. You keep shipping. You tell yourself you're adjusting.

Meanwhile, something is quietly eroding: the craft satisfaction that got you into this field.

The thing is — **velocity and craft are not the same thing.**

You can ship faster and understand less. The gap between what you're producing and what you're learning is the specific pain of AI fatigue.

**[→ Read: Who Am I Without My Code?](https://clearing-ai.com/developer-identity.html)**

### One Thing You Can Do This Week

**The Explanation Requirement.**

Before you commit any AI suggestion, explain it to yourself out loud (or in a comment). Why does this fix work? What would happen if I did it differently?

Don't accept the answer. Demand the explanation.

This one practice does two things:
1. It keeps your "why" understanding active
2. It tells your brain this is still YOUR code, not AI's code you're approving

It's a small thing. It takes 30 extra seconds per suggestion.

Try it for three days. Notice what changes.
