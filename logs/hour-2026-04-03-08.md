### Thread Structure

**Tweet 1:**
Something engineers tell me after taking our AI Fatigue Quiz:

"I don't recognize the code I ship anymore."

Not "the code is bad." 
Not "the architecture is wrong."
Just... it's not quite *theirs*.

Sunday nights are the hardest. 

↓
**Tweet 2:**
You spent years learning to feel your way through a codebase. The hunch that says "this module is where the bug lives." The instinct that something is architecturally wrong before you've proven it.

That feeling took 5-10 years to develop.

AI coding tools are very good at answering questions. They're systematically eroding the instinct that lets senior engineers ask the *right* questions.

↓
**Tweet 3:**
The result: you ship more code than ever. You feel less of a developer than you did two years ago.

This isn't imposter syndrome. 
Imposter syndrome says "I'm not good enough." 
This says "I used to be good at this and I'm not sure I am anymore."

Different pain. Different solution.

↓
**Tweet 4:**
Here's what the engineers who recovered have in common:

• Protected no-AI coding sessions (1-2h, weekly minimum)
• The "Explanation Requirement" — no AI output until they've predicted the answer first
• A quarterly "Rebuild from Scratch" challenge (even a small feature, built without AI)
• Sunday night rituals that have nothing to do with code

↓
**Tweet 5:**
If you're a senior engineer who feels like something's quietly slipping — it probably is. You're not broken. The environment changed. Your instincts are still there; they just need recalibrating.
