## Tweet 1
AI saves you time. But it takes something else: context.

Every time an AI solves a problem for you, the context you built solving it fades.

The problem isn't the AI. It's what your brain doesn't get to consolidate.

---

## Tweet 2
Gloria Mark's research at UC Irvine: after an interruption, it takes 23 minutes to fully rebuild your focus.

Now imagine 40 AI suggestions a day. Each one: a micro-interruption. Each resolution: a shallow win.

Your brain never gets to the deep stuff because the deep stuff keeps getting... outsourced.

---

## Tweet 3
The hidden cost isn't time. It's cognitive residue.

The mental model you would have built solving that problem yourself? Gone.
The pattern recognition you'd have developed? Gone.
The confidence that comes from "I figured this out"? Also gone.

You shipped faster. You learned less.

---

## Tweet 4
Here's what nobody talks about: AI makes you competent without capable.

You can review AI-generated code and think "I understand this." You probably don't — not at the level where you could have written it.

This is competence illusion. And it's insidious because you don't know it's happening.

---

## Tweet 5
The fix isn't using less AI. It's being deliberate about which problems you hand off.

Healthiest engineers I talk to: they use AI to *teach* themselves, not to *replace* themselves.

The question isn't "should I use AI?" It's "will I still be growing after this?"
