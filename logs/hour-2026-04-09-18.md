A 3,500-4,000 word deep-dive explaining why AI-assisted learning paradoxically exhausts developers more than traditional learning — and what to do about it.

### Content Outline

**1. Opening (300 words)**
- The tutorial loop phenomenon: watch → code along → feel like you understand → try solo → fail → repeat
- Why this feels worse than traditional learning (you *should* be learning faster with AI)
- The hidden cost: passive recognition vs active retrieval

**2. Why AI Makes Learning Exhausting (800 words)**
- **The comprehension illusion**: AI explains in seconds, you nod, but memory consolidation takes effort — illusion of understanding
- **The shortcut pathway**: solving with AI → bypasses productive struggle → skill formation gap
- **The speed mismatch**: AI delivers answers instantly vs brain's ~7-second encoding window
- **The verification burden**: now you must also evaluate whether the AI was *right* — adds cognitive load layer
- **The ambient knowledge myth**: "I'm around AI so I'm learning" — passive exposure ≠ active encoding

**3. The Learning Stack Problem (500 words)**
- Engineers now have 3 jobs: write code + verify AI output + learn the underlying (never time for #3)
- "Learning in layers" is broken — AI collapses layers but doesn't transfer them
- The half-life problem: tutorial learned today → obsolete in 6 months → repeat forever

**4. 7 Signs You're in AI Learning Burnout (400 words)**
- Finishing tutorials but can't build from scratch
- Feeling guilty when not using AI to learn
- Resetting the same "learning project" repeatedly
- Searching for "best way to learn X with AI" more than actually learning X
- Physical fatigue after "learning days"
- Confusion between AI's knowledge and your knowledge
- The tutorial paradox: more resources, less retention

**5. The Tutorial Paradox (500 words)**
- YouTube/Sololearn/ChatGPT: infinite free tutorials = infinite learning = infinite exhaustion
- Choice overload: which tutorial is best? → decision fatigue before learning even starts
- The "one more tutorial" trap: procrastination disguised as productivity
- Why tutorials work for novices but collapse for experienced devs (expertise reversal effect, Kalyuga)

**6. What Actually Works: Evidence-Based Learning in the AI Era (700 words)**
- **Retrieval practice over re-reading**: close the book, write from memory, then check (Bjork)
- **The Explanation Requirement**: explain what you just learned to someone (or yourself) without AI
- **No-AI learning blocks**: 90 minutes/week of pure struggle — productive friction is the point
- **Spaced repetition for retention**: review after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 21 days (Ebbinghaus)
- **Build for the struggle**: intentionally choose hard projects where AI helps 30%, not 90%
- **Track what you actually know**: weekly self-test, not just tutorial completion

**7. For Teams and Managers (300 words)**
- Stop mandating AI tool adoption in learning programs
- Create protected "learn-from-scratch" time
- Evaluate learning outcomes, not tool usage metrics
- The manager's role: model learning visibly

**8. FAQ Accordion (6 Q&As)**
- Q: Isn't AI supposed to make learning faster?
- Q: I finish tutorials but can't code the project — what do I do?
- Q: How do I know if I'm learning or just watching?
- Q: Is it bad that I still don't know how to build things from scratch?
- Q: How much should I rely on AI when learning new things?
- Q: What about learning in a work context with deadlines?
