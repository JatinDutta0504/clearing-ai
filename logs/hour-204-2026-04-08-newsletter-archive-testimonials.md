## The Compounding Problem

The engineers reporting the highest fatigue share one pattern: they have 4+ AI tools active in their workflow. Not one or two — four, five, sometimes six. Copilot for code. ChatGPT for research. Claude for code review. Midjourney for design specs. Perplexity for documentation. Gemini for meetings.

On paper: productivity. In practice: cognitive bankruptcy.

Every tool switch is a context switch. Gloria Mark's research at UC Irvine found it takes an average of 23 minutes to fully recover focus after a single interruption. If you're doing 8-10 tool switches per hour during a work day, your brain is in permanent recovery mode by 11 AM.

The compounding isn't linear. It's exponential.

---

## The Pattern No One Names

Here's what the data shows:

**Engineers using 4+ AI tools were 2.3x more likely to report:**
- "I can't remember the last time I built something without AI"
- "I feel like a middleman, not a builder"
- "My skills are declining and I can name specific examples"

**They were also 40% less likely to identify what was wrong.**

The tool overload creates its own fog. You stop noticing the decline because you're too busy staying afloat.

---

## What Actually Helps (The Research)

You already know "use less AI." That's not realistic for most teams.

The sustainable path: **one tool at a time, fully processed.**

Pick one AI tool as your primary. When it gives you output, close all other AI tools and process that output completely — understand it, question it, rebuild parts of it — before moving to the next task.

It's not about AI being bad. It's about the cognitive cost of rapid tool switching being hidden. Once you see it, you can't unsee it.
