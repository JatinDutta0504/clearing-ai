The Google Effect (Sparrow et al., 2011): when we know information is externally stored, we mentally compress it. We remember less, and feel like we know more.

AI tools are Google × 100. The compression is severe.

The dangerous version: you stop building the model AND feel confident you have it.

The Expertise Reversal Effect (Kalyuga et al., 2003): interventions that help novices actually hinder experts.

This is why 6-year engineers report more AI fatigue than juniors. The asymmetry is real.
