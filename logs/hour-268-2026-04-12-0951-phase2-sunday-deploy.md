### Twitter Thread #12 — "The Middleman Problem" — Deploy Sunday 12-2 PM PDT

**6-tweet thread:**
1. You ship more code than ever. But when you sit down to write it — blank. This is the middleman problem.
2. Traditional engineering: you → the work → the outcome. Every decision yours.
3. With AI: you → AI → the work → the outcome. You review. You ship. The understanding isn't fully yours.
4. The exhaustion is responsibility without understanding. Different from imposter syndrome — it's functional.
5. 5 signs you're in it: explains product but not implementation, debugging feels like QA, couldn't reproduce from scratch...
6. The way out: maintain one thread of work that's fully yours. The Explanation Requirement. clearing-ai.com/quiz

### Twitter Thread #13 — "The Sunday Night Reckoning" — Deploy Sunday 9-11 AM PDT

**6-tweet thread:**
1. Every Sunday night, a specific kind of engineer opens their laptop and feels something they can't quite name. 🧵
2. The thing they're actually doing: running a cognitive audit. Did I learn anything? Did my skills get sharper? Am I still the engineer I was 6 months ago?
3. The pattern: AI makes you faster at small questions. Small questions = less cognitive work. Less work = weaker pattern recognition. Weaker patterns = smaller questions. Repeat. This is the loop.
4. The Sunday Night Reckoning: the loop ran long enough to produce a signal — "I don't feel like an engineer anymore." Not burnout. Not imposter syndrome. The middleman problem.
5. Engineers navigating this well ask one question every Sunday without AI. Not a test. Just: what does my brain actually know right now? The answer is data.
6. Free AI Fatigue Quiz — 5 questions, 90 seconds, no signup. Might give you language for what you're noticing. clearing-ai.com
