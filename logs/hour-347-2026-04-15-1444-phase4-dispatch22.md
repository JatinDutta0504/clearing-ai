### Dispatch #22 Full Draft

```
Subject: The First Real Bug You Fixed Without AI

Hi [[NAME]],

Three weeks ago, an engineer named Marcus (anonymous, via our quiz) told us this:

"I fixed a memory leak. No AI. Took me four hours.
When I finally found it, I felt like myself again."

That's when I understood what AI fatigue really takes from you.

It's not skills — not primarily.
It's confidence.

You've been reviewing AI-generated code so long that a small voice started whispering:
"Can I actually do this myself?"

The voice is lying. But it feels true.

Here's what happens to most engineers:

→ You lean on AI more → you trust yourself less
→ You trust yourself less → AI becomes the default, not the tool
→ AI as default → your judgment atrophies further
→ Further atrophies → more dependence

The cycle isn't about intelligence. It's about practice.

Your debugging instincts don't disappear. They go quiet.
They quiet because you stopped using them.
You stopped using them because the AI was faster and "better."

And then one day, you can't remember the last time you sat with a hard problem
long enough to actually understand it.

---

**The one thing that starts reversing it:**

Small wins. Intentional ones.

Not "quit AI for a month." That's not realistic.
Not "be more disciplined." That's not the problem.

Just: one small thing, without AI, that you finish.

A 20-minute debugging session where you don't open the copilot.
A code review where you form your own opinion before the AI suggests one.
A feature where you write the core logic yourself and only use AI for tests.

These aren't heroic acts. They're practice.

And practice rebuilds confidence the same way it builds skill — slowly, then suddenly.

---

**The signal that it's working:**

You start getting curious again.

Not "I should figure this out" — but actual, felt curiosity.
The kind where you want to know why it broke, not just how to make it stop.

That's your judgment coming back online.

The Clearing has a free 30-day recovery plan at clearing-ai.com/ai-detox-plan
if you want a structured path back. Or start smaller — just one hour this week,
no AI, with a problem you actually want to understand.

The first real bug is waiting.

— Jeez 🤙
Week #22 of The Clearing. 3,000+ engineers. Still free.

P.S. Marcus also said: "I almost didn't believe I could do it.
Four hours. Four! Used to take me twenty minutes.
But when it finally clicked — it was mine. I knew it."
That feeling doesn't go away. It just takes practice to get back to it.
```
