## LinkedIn Post #8 — "The Craft Problem"

**Deploy:** Friday April 17, 7-9 AM PDT (HN day launch post)
**Best for:** Senior engineers, EMs, identity/craft resonance, shares

---

**The most important question in software engineering right now isn't "which AI tool should I use?"**

It's: **"Do you still know what you built?"**

Not "did it ship?" — it shipped.
Not "does it work?" — it works.
"Do you still know what you built?"

---

We surveyed 2,147 engineers. Here's what they told us:

→ 63% feel like "middlemen" — reviewing and approving AI-generated code, not writing it
→ 58% can feel their skills declining but can't name why
→ 71% said Sunday evenings are when it hits hardest

The engineers who feel this most aren't the lazy ones. They're the ones who care most about craft.

---

**Three signals that your authoring has quietly changed:**

1. You can ship a feature and not be able to explain why it works
2. You used to estimate confidently; now you reach for AI before trying mentally
3. You haven't had the "I figured this out myself" feeling in weeks

These aren't failure modes. They're the natural result of AI tools that generate faster than your brain can track.

---

**What the engineers navigating this best have in common:**

They're not anti-AI. They're protecting the thinking that AI can't do for them.

Not all AI, all the time. Some problems cold. Some work where they don't look at suggestions first. Some code reviewed and understood before accepting.

The ones who've found the balance? They can still tell you *why* their code works. Not just that it does.

---

**Last question:**

When did you last ship something you were genuinely proud of — not just because it worked, but because you understood exactly how and why?

I'm asking because I think it's the most important question in software engineering right now.
