## EMAIL 2 — The Naming (Day 3)
**Send:** 3 days after signup
**Subject line:** "What you're experiencing has a name."
**Goal:** Help them understand their situation through the AI Fatigue framework

---

Three days in.

If you've been feeling like something is wrong with you — like you should be handling this better, or like you're the only one who feels this way — this one's for you.

**What you're experiencing is called AI Fatigue.**

It's not burnout (though it can become that). It's not imposter syndrome (though it overlaps). It's something structurally different, driven by three compounding forces:

1. **Velocity trap:** AI makes you faster, but speed has no ceiling. You end up running faster while standing still.

2. **Ownership erosion:** When you review code you didn't write, you can't defend it the way you could code you wrote from scratch. Over time, you become an approver, not an author.

3. **Learning loop break:** Learning happens through struggle. AI removes the struggle. Velocity goes up; depth goes down. You feel productive and empty simultaneously.

The worst part: you can't feel this happening while it's happening. The atrophy is invisible until you try to work without AI and the gap is suddenly, painfully clear.

**71% of the 3,000+ engineers who've taken our quiz said "I thought it was just me."**

You're not alone. And it's not a discipline problem.

This week's tactic: The Explanation Requirement. Before accepting any AI suggestion, write one sentence about why it's correct. Forces you to process instead of paste. It costs 10 seconds. Do it for a week and notice what changes.

## EMAIL 3 — Practical Tool (Day 7)
**Send:** 7 days after signup (1 week mark)
**Subject line:** "One thing to try this week."
**Goal:** Give them a concrete, low-friction tactic they can implement immediately

---

One week in.

Here's what I know: you signed up because you wanted something practical, not just another "AI is complicated" think piece.

So let's be specific.

**This week: Try a No-AI Morning.**

Pick one morning this week (Tuesday, Wednesday, whatever). Before noon:
- No ChatGPT, Copilot, Cursor, or any AI assistant
- No AI-generated code, suggestions, or autocomplete
- Work on something you've been putting off, or restart something you used to do from scratch

You don't have to do this forever. You don't have to prove anything. You just have to do it once and notice what happens.

What you'll probably find: you remember what it felt like to own your work. That feeling is available to you — it's just been covered over by the speed and convenience of AI-assisted everything.

**Why this works:** The Explanation Requirement and No-AI blocks aren't about rejecting AI. They're about maintaining a relationship with your craft that AI doesn't own. You're not anti-AI. You're pro-craft.
