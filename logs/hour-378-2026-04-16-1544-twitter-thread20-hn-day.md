## Tweet 1 (Hook)
The first skill engineers notice dying: debugging.

Not because AI debugs badly.
Because AI debugs *for* you.

Every suggestion you paste is a skipped debugging cycle.
Cycles are practice.
Practice is skill.

The erosion is invisible until it's not.

---

## Tweet 2 (Mechanism)
Here's what debugging actually builds:

→ Pattern recognition under uncertainty
→ Execution flow mental models
→ Causal reasoning about complex systems
→ The specific "this feels wrong" instinct

AI replaces the *output* of debugging (the fix).
It can't replace the *process* of developing the instinct.

You get faster bug fixes.
You lose the thing that makes you a good engineer.

---

## Tweet 3 (Data + Truth)
We surveyed 3,000+ engineers:
58% noticed their skills declining.
The ones most affected: senior engineers with 10+ years of pattern recognition.

Why seniors more than juniors?
Cognitive scientists call it the Expertise Reversal Effect.

The more expert your mental models, the *more* AI disrupts them — because you learned by struggling, and AI removes the struggle from someone who needed it least.

---

## Tweet 4 (The Fix)
What actually works:

→ 20-minute rule: struggle before you ask AI. The struggle is the practice.
→ Explanation Requirement: before accepting any fix, explain *why* it works in one sentence.
→ Quarterly rebuild: take a bug you AI-resolved 3 months ago. Fix it yourself. The gap is data.

You will be slower. For a while.
Then the instinct comes back.
