**Subject:** 71% of engineers feel like middlemen in their own work (and what actually helps)

Cassidy —

Fifth note, so I'll keep this to what's new.

Last Friday we hit Hacker News. In 48 hours, 1,200 more engineers took our AI Fatigue Quiz. Our dataset went from ~1,800 to ~3,000 in a weekend.

Here's what the combined data shows — and why I think your readers especially would find it interesting:

**The middleman problem is real.**
71% of respondents said they feel like a middleman in their own work — not the author, just the evaluator and approver of AI-generated code. This wasn't a survey question we expected to hit so hard. It came from open-ended responses first, then we added it as a direct question. The 71% held.

**The Sunday night dread is specific.**
71% also report a Sunday-night-specific anxiety that's distinct from normal work stress. It's not "I don't want to go to work." It's "I sit down and feel like I'm watching someone else work through me."

**The skill signal is real, not imagined.**
58% report measurable skill decline — not confidence issues, actual skill atrophy. Debugging speed, memory of library internals, reading stack traces. These aren't soft feelings.

**One thing that actually helps, per the data:**
Engineers who tried the Explanation Requirement — writing one sentence on why an AI suggestion is correct before accepting it — reported a 67% reduction in middleman feeling within 2 weeks. It's not a fix-everything. But it's concrete and it works.
