**Subject:** After the storm 🌊

**Opening (1-2 sentences):**
If you arrived here from Hacker News this week — welcome. You're now one of 3,000+ engineers who took the AI Fatigue Quiz in the past 30 days. Pull up a chair.

**Section 1: What the thread got right (and what it didn't)**
HN nailed the diagnosis: something real is happening to engineers' sense of ownership over their code. The middleman framing landed. What the thread couldn't capture: the day-to-day texture of working that way for months, and what it takes to change course.

**Section 2: The gap between knowing and doing**
Recognizing "I'm a middleman now" and actually doing something about it are different skills. Here's what we've heard from tier 2 and tier 3 quiz takers in the past week: the moment of recognition is validating. The week after is harder. You know what's happening. You're still doing it.

**Section 3: One thing this week**
Small correction over time beats overhaul. This week, try one thing: before you accept any AI suggestion, finish this sentence in your head — "I adjusted this because..." Just the explanation. Not even out loud. The act of completing the explanation restores the ownership loop, even before you act on it.

**Section 4: What's coming**
Next week: the 30-day plan gets a new section on rebuild practices — specific exercises engineers use to get specific skills back. Debugging, estimation, architecture reading. Not "use AI less." Which specific skill you want to recover.

**Closing:**
If the quiz landed for you, share it. The engineers who most need this are often the ones who think they're just tired. Forward this to someone who's been quiet lately. — SJ
