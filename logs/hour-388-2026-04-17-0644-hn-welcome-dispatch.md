## THE HN WELCOME DISPATCH — Special Issue

**Subject:** Welcome from clearing-ai.com — what 3,000 engineers told us

---

If you're coming from Hacker News today: welcome. Here's the short version of what we built and why.

A year ago I watched a senior engineer friend stare at a diff at 6pm and say "I don't know if I wrote this or if I just approved it." He wasn't burned out. He wasn't lazy. He'd been shipping code every day for 12 years. Something had quietly changed.

That's the moment I started building clearing-ai.com.

---

### What 3,000 engineers told us

We put a 5-question quiz on the site. Here's what the data says:

**71% feel like middlemen.** Code ships. They don't feel like they built it.

**63% feel like strangers to their own work.** There's production code they couldn't write from scratch.

**58% notice their skills declining** in specific, nameable areas — debugging, estimation, reading code they didn't write.

**44% have considered leaving the profession entirely.**

These aren't burnout numbers. The engineers writing in most often were senior ICs with 10-15 years of experience. Not because they couldn't keep up — because they could feel the gap between what they were outputting and what they understood.

---

### The middleman problem

The pattern has a name now: the middleman problem.

AI generates → you review → you approve → it ships
You do this 10-20 times a day.
After 6 months: the code shipped. The understanding? Gone.

The dangerous part isn't the code that ships. It's what happens at 2am when something breaks and you didn't write it — and you can't feel your way through it the way you used to.

---

### One thing you can do today

Before you merge anything AI helped write, finish this sentence in your head:

"I adjusted this because..."

"The route handles auth because I checked the token validation logic."
"I changed the cache key because I traced the invalidation sequence."

That sentence — completed, not performed — rebuilds the ownership loop AI quietly dissolved. It takes 10 seconds. Do it today.
