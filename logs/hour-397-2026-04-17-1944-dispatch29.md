## Dispatch #29 Draft: "The Sneakiest Part"

**Subject:** The sneakiest part of AI fatigue

**Preheader:** It doesn't announce itself. That's what makes it different from burnout.

---

**Opening:**
If you arrived from Hacker News this week: welcome. You're now one of 3,000+ engineers who took the AI Fatigue Quiz this month.

Pull up a chair. I want to tell you something honest about what comes next.

**Section 1: The sneakiest part**
Burnout has a crisis moment. You know it's happening because something breaks — a deadline, a relationship, your sleep, your patience. There's a clear line before and after.

AI fatigue doesn't work that way.

It sneaks in. You don't notice because the metrics look fine. You're shipping. You're closing tickets. Your velocity numbers are good. And somewhere in the background, quietly:

— You stopped being able to explain your own code
— You stopped feeling the satisfaction of building something hard
— You stopped learning the things you used to learn
— The Sunday night dread started feeling normal

None of those things fire a warning shot. They're individually dismissible. Together they're a pattern.

The quiz asks about all four. That's not accidental.

---

**Section 2: What experienced engineers tell us**
Three thousand quiz takers later, the most common response to "what made you take the quiz" isn't a crisis. It's a quiet noticing:

"I took it because I couldn't remember the last time I built something from scratch."

Not a breakdown. Not a burnout. A noticing.

That's the sneakiness. You're not broken. You're just slowly disappearing from your own work.

---

**Section 3: One honest question**
Here's the question I keep coming back to:

"If you had to build your current project's core feature from scratch — no AI, no reference code, no Stack Overflow — could you do it in the time it would take you to prompt for it?"

Not "should you." Not "is this bad." Just: could you?

If yes — you're okay. Keep paying attention.
If no — you're in the right place. And knowing the answer is already the first step.

---

**Section 4: What happens next**
If you're new here, here's the honest roadmap:

Week 1: The Quiz → Your tier page → One tactic (try the Explanation Requirement)
Week 2–4: The 30-Day Plan → Rebuild one skill you lost
Ongoing: The Dispatch every Sunday → One honest thing about AI fatigue

The people who get the most out of this are the ones who stop waiting for a crisis moment and start paying attention to the creep.

---

**Closing:**
If this landed for you, forward it to the engineer who's been quiet lately. The ones who most need this are usually the ones who think they're just tired.

— SJ
