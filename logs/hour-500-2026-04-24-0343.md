- **Layer 1: Surface comprehension** — the fluency illusion (AI explains → you understand → you think you own it)
- **Layer 2: Successful retrieval** — why struggle builds durable knowledge and AI bypasses it
- **The 38% self-assessment failure** — from 2,147 engineer survey (systematic overestimation of own skills under AI use)
- **Three hidden costs:** debugging erosion first, design sense goes quiet, the interview problem
- **Four practices:** weekly no-AI self-check / explanation test / design one thing from scratch / track skill gaps

## Why this topic

The competence illusion is the unspoken thing at the center of AI fatigue. It's not about laziness or burnout — it's a structural feature of AI-assisted work. Engineers feel it but can't name it, which makes it feel like personal failure. Naming it is the first step.
