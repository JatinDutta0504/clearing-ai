### From Hour 73 (1 comment — NEW this window):
**r/BurnOut** — "Is this burnout or am I just tired?" thread
> The thing you're describing — the gap between "I'm doing a lot" and "I'm actually learning or growing" — has a name: AI fatigue. It's distinct from burnout, though they overlap.
>
> The quick distinction: burnout is about exhaustion from work volume and emotional demands. AI fatigue is about a specific kind of cognitive displacement — you're doing more while understanding less, shipping code you don't fully own, learning at a fraction of the rate you used to.
>
> A few signals that point to AI fatigue specifically:
> - You can describe what you shipped last week but can't remember how any of it actually works
> - Code review feels like gatekeeping rather than collaboration (you're just checking, not understanding)
> - You used to have "aha" moments during implementation — those have disappeared
> - The thought of rebuilding something from scratch feels genuinely frightening rather than challenging
>
> The recovery is different too. Burnout needs rest and boundary-setting. AI fatigue needs re-engagement — deliberate friction, explaining things back to yourself, building things without AI help even when it's slower.
>
> If any of this resonates, there's a quiz at clearing-ai.com that might help you name what you're experiencing. Sometimes just having the vocabulary for it helps.
