## Tweet 2 (Cognitive Load Theory)
Cognitive Load Theory (Sweller, 1988): your working memory handles ~4 items at once.

AI tools don't reduce cognitive load — they ADD a new layer:
→ prompt formulation
→ output integration
→ credibility checking
→ context-switching cost

You're not just working. You're meta-working.

---

## Tweet 3 (The 23-Minute Gap)
23-minute recovery gap (Gloria Mark, UC Irvine):

Every time you context-switch from deep work to an AI tool, it takes 23 minutes to fully re-engage.

Most engineers switch 15-20x/hour.

That's 5.75-7.6 hours of cognitive recovery debt per day.

You can't out-rest a structural problem.

---

## Tweet 4 (The Compounding Trap)
The trap: AI makes you FEEL productive while accelerating skill atrophy.

You shipped code. You didn't build it.

After 6 months, the gap between "what you know" and "what you shipped" becomes:
→ identity-threatening
→ confidence-eroding
→ professionally dangerous

The velocity felt like growth. It wasn't.
