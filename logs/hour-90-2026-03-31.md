### Reddit Comments for HN Day (Deploy Wed-Thu 9am-2pm PDT)

**r/programming** — "Why does AI coding assistance feel more exhausting than normal coding?"
- The mechanism: attention residue. Every time you switch between writing and evaluating AI suggestions, your brain keeps a "process remnant" of the previous task active. Leroy (2009): 23 minutes to fully re-engage. With AI you switch constantly, with almost zero friction. You never fully arrive in any task.
- Link: clearing-ai.com/attention-residue.html

**r/webdev** — "Am I the only one who feels like AI tools make me a worse developer?"
- You're not. The science of "automation bias" (Parasuraman) explains why: when AI handles the active problem-solving, the passive monitoring you do doesn't maintain the same skills. Your brain's competence circuits don't fire the same way. The "I should be keeping up" feeling isn't weakness — it's a documented cognitive phenomenon with real solutions.
- Link: clearing-ai.com/the-science-of-ai-fatigue.html

**r/ExperiencedDevs** — "How do you deal with feeling like you're not learning anymore?"
- This is the productive struggle problem (Bjork's "desirable difficulties"). The struggle to solve something hard is the mechanism through which your brain marks it as worth remembering. When AI removes the struggle, it removes the learning signal too. The fix isn't more AI — it's protected time without it.
- Link: clearing-ai.com/skill-atrophy.html

**r/cscareerquestions** — "Is AI fatigue a real thing or am I just burned out?"
- Real thing, different mechanism. Burnout is occupational exhaustion from chronic work stress over months/years. AI fatigue is specific: it's how AI tools interact with working memory limits, attention residue, automation bias, and identity threat responses. The exhaustion pattern is different — more diffuse, less tied to specific deadlines, more tied to the feeling of not being fully present in your own work.
- Link: clearing-ai.com/burnout-vs-fatigue.html
