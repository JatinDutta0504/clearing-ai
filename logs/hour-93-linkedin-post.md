Something strange started happening to engineers on my team around 2023.

They were shipping more code than ever. Velocity was up. But they'd stay late not because they were excited — because they felt like frauds.

They'd say things like:
- "I can't tell if I actually solved that problem or if AI did"
- "I used to know how everything worked. Now I just know how to prompt"
- "Sunday nights feel like a dread I can't explain"

I watched good engineers start to disengage. Not because they were burned out in the old sense. Something new was happening.

---

**What we built**

I spent the last year building clearing-ai.com with two friends who felt the same thing.

It's a free resource for engineers experiencing what we're calling AI fatigue — a distinct condition from burnout, with different causes and different solutions.

We built:
→ A 5-question AI Fatigue Quiz (3 min)
→ A 30-day structured recovery plan
→ 70+ pages of guides covering identity, skill atrophy, attention, recovery
→ A daily check-in tool and deep work timer

Not "try harder" advice. Not productivity hacks. Real frameworks grounded in cognitive science.

---

**What 3,000+ engineers told us**

After 3,000+ quiz completions, the patterns are clear:

🧠 63% feel like "middlemen" — passing AI output through without adding value
📉 58% notice measurable skill decline in areas they used to own
🧪 71% treat their job like taking a test — completing tasks, not building mastery
😰 44% have considered leaving the industry entirely

These aren't lazy engineers. These are the most productive engineers in history, burning out on a new kind of hamster wheel.

---

**The uncomfortable question**

If you're a manager: when was the last time you asked your team how they're actually doing — not just whether they're shipping?

If you're an IC: do you still feel like a craftsman, or like a sophisticated middleware?

The engineers who are going to thrive in the next 5 years aren't the ones who use AI the most.

They're the ones who've figured out how to use it without being used by it.
