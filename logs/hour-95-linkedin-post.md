We asked 2,047 engineers a simple question:

*"Do you recognize the code you ship as yours?"*

63% said something that isn't quite yes and isn't quite no.

They're in the middle — watching code go out that they didn't quite write, 
for users they'll never quite meet, for problems they didn't quite define.

Not burned out. Not fine. Middleman.

---

Here's what else the data showed:

71% describe their AI workflow as "taking a test" 
rather than "building something."

58% noticed measurable skill decline in the past 6 months.

44% are actively considering leaving engineering because of it.

---

I built clearing-ai.com because a friend said something 
I couldn't stop thinking about:

*"I can ship a feature in 2 hours that used to take me 2 days.
 And I feel nothing. And I can't figure out why that makes me angry."*

That's not laziness. That's agency erosion — a quiet gap between 
the work and the worker. And the industry keeps telling people 
to just adapt.

---

What actually helps:

→ Protected no-AI blocks (2h/week minimum)
→ The Explanation Requirement (say why before accepting AI suggestions)
→ Quarterly full-build challenges (build something small, completely alone)
→ Naming it — giving the pattern a real term
