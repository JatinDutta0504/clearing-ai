## Page Structure

**Title:** "A Quiet Manifesto for Engineers Who Still Give a Damn"
**Word count:** ~4,000 words (essay, not a listicle)

### Sections:
1. **Opening provocation** — "You didn't imagine this." The quiet dissonance engineers feel but can't articulate.
2. **What we're actually losing** — Not jobs. Agency. Craft. The point of the work.
3. **The seduction** — AI tools aren't the enemy. The ease IS the trap.
4. **The measurement problem** — Why shipping fast became the only metric that matters.
5. **What "good work" actually requires** — Deliberate struggle, earned intuition, real outcomes.
6. **The industry lie** — "Adapt or die" is a False Dichotomy. You can adapt AND maintain standards.
7. **What we stand for** — The Clearing's position, clearly stated. No corporate hedging.
8. **A practical refusal** — Not Luddism. Intentionality. Choosing when to use tools and when not to.
9. **The long game** — Your career is 40 years. The tools will change a hundred times. Your judgment is permanent.
