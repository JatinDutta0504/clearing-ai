## Defensive Response Document — 5 Likely Criticisms

### "This is just burnout rebranded"
Burnout is about work volume and emotional exhaustion. AI fatigue is specifically about the *learning loop being broken* — you stop acquiring the skills you're evaluated on, your expertise stops growing, and your identity as a "developer who can figure things out" erodes. They overlap significantly and can compound each other, but the mechanism is different. See: https://clearing-ai.com/burnout-vs-fatigue.html

### "You're just anti-AI"
The site is explicitly not anti-AI. The framing is: use AI intentionally, protect the learning loop, and don't let velocity metrics replace depth. The same engineers driving AI adoption at work are the ones describing loss of craft — that's not a technology rejection, it's a craft preservation argument.

### "Why would I trust a quiz?"
Fair. It's not diagnostic. It's a structured self-reflection tool — questions designed to make you pause and reflect on patterns you might have been ignoring. The value is in the reflection, not the score.

### "2000 people is not a sample size"
Correct — the quiz spread organically, not through a controlled study. The data is directional: 63% identifying with "code shipped but I don't own it" is a signal, not a proof. The cited research (Gloria Mark, Sweller, Csikszentmihalyi) is real — these are the academic frameworks that explain what engineers are describing.

### "This is just a marketing funnel"
The site has zero ads, no affiliate links, no paid tier. It's built and maintained by engineers who experienced this themselves. The goal is awareness and recovery, not monetization. If that changes, it will be stated clearly.
