## 🟡 TODAY — Reddit Comments 7 & 8 (May 8-14 Pack — LAST TWO)

### Comment 7 — r/Burnout
**Due: TODAY (Mon May 12) — REDDIT SCHEDULE DAY 4**

> The exhaustion that doesn't map to physical effort is usually cognitive load — your brain working hard to manage something that's supposed to reduce your cognitive burden.
>
> With AI tools specifically: using them well requires simultaneously understanding the problem, evaluating the AI's suggestion, tracking whether it integrates correctly into your mental model, and staying aware of the overall context. Four parallel processes at once. Your working memory holds maybe four items.
>
> That's not burnout from quantity of work. It's burnout from cognitive overhead — the mental cost of compensating for the very tool that's supposed to reduce friction.
>
> The practical implication: batching AI usage rather than continuous use tends to help. One decision point at a time, rather than continuous parallel processing.
>
> The "can't pinpoint why" is part of the pattern. It's hard to name because the tools are supposed to be helping.

**Thread context:** Someone says "Feeling exhausted but can't pinpoint why"

### Comment 8 — r/ExperiencedDevs
**Due: Wed May 14 — REDDIT SCHEDULE DAY 5**

> Not a senior dev but I've talked to enough to notice a pattern in the answers that work:
>
> The skill they protect isn't the technical skill. It's the "knowing what to ask" skill — the judgment about whether something is worth doing, whether a problem is actually solved, whether an approach will scale. That's not in the code. That's in the experience of having made those wrong and learned from it.
>
> AI can generate the solution. It can't generate the taste that tells you which solution to pursue.
>
> What they're protecting is the judgment muscle, not the generation muscle. The generation gets offloaded. The judgment stays.
>
> The second thing: debugging without AI. Not because it's faster — it's not. But because the slow debug process rebuilds an intuition about the system that you lose when you just paste errors into a chat.
>
> Both of these are documented in the research at clearing-ai.com for anyone who wants the framework.

**Thread context:** "Senior devs — what's one skill you refuse to let AI handle?"
