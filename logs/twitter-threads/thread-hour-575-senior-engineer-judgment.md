Thread #42: 3 signs your technical judgment is eroding from AI dependency

---

1/ The most dangerous thing happening to senior engineers right now isn't looking incompetent.

It's looking completely competent while your judgment quietly atrophies.

---

2/ Sign 1: Patterns that should feel wrong don't anymore.

You used to read a PR and immediately sense the architectural problem.
Now you approve it because the AI didn't flag it.

Your gut has been outsourced to a tool that optimizes for "working" not "right."

---

3/ Sign 2: You can't explain why the code works.

You can ship. You can debug. You can ship more.

But if someone asks you to explain the mechanism — the why behind the what — you describe what the tool did, not what's happening.

This is invisible. Nobody asks. You don't notice.

Until the interview. Or the incident. Or the architecture decision you make wrong.

---

4/ Sign 3: You trust the tool before you trust the team.

When there's a disagreement between your senior engineer and the AI suggestion, what's your first instinct?

If it's "the AI is probably right" — that's the moment you need to pay attention to.

This is the creepiest one. I've talked to staff engineers who catch themselves doing this and it scares them.

---

5/ The path back isn't "stop using AI."

It's: use it as a sparring partner, not an authority.

No-AI coding sessions. Explanation requirement before accepting any suggestion. Teaching the team what you just learned — not just shipping the result.

Your judgment is worth protecting. It's the one thing that can't be automated.
