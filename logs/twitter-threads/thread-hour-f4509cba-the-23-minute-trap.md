## Thread (8 tweets)

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Every time you switch from coding to asking an AI a question, your brain needs 23 minutes to get back to full focus.

Most engineers switch 20+ times a day.

Do the math.

---

**Tweet 2 (The research):**
Gloria Mark at UC Irvine studied knowledge workers for years.

Her finding: after a distraction, it takes an average of 23 minutes and 15 seconds to return to full cognitive focus.

Not 2 minutes. Not "a quick context switch." 23 minutes.

---

**Tweet 3 (Why AI is worse than other distractions):**
Regular interruptions (Slack, email) at least have a clear "done" moment.

AI conversations don't.

You finish one query. The output looks almost right. You adjust the prompt. You compare. You re-prompt. You paste the result.

That's not one interruption. That's a 15-minute multi-switch event with no clean boundary.

---

**Tweet 4 (The compounding math):**
If you have 20 AI-assisted sessions in a day:
- Traditional work: 20 × 5 min recovery = 100 min of lost focus
- AI work: 20 × 15 min recovery = 300 min (5 hours) of cognitive debt

That's right. AI might be costing you 5 hours of deep focus per day.

---

**Tweet 5 (The cruel part):**
Your output metrics look fine. You shipped 8 PRs today. Your standup looked good.

But the 5 hours of cognitive debt you accumulated? It's invisible. It shows up as:
- Sunday dread
- Feeling busy but not proud
- That vague sense that you're competent but not sharp

You can't see the debt. Until it compounds.

---

**Tweet 6 (The fix isn't "use less AI"):**
The fix: batch AI work.

Instead of: code → AI query → code → AI query → code
Do: 90 min pure coding → 20 min AI batch session → 90 min pure coding

The AI session becomes one intentional context switch instead of 10 accidental ones.

---

**Tweet 7 (The 90-minute rule):**
Your brain can't go deep in 30-minute chunks.

90 minutes is the minimum time to reach deep focus (Csikszentmihalyi's flow research).

Before AI: you'd naturally hit 90 minutes because switching was hard.
After AI: each prompt breaks your focus before you ever get there.

Batch your AI queries. Protect your 90-minute blocks.
