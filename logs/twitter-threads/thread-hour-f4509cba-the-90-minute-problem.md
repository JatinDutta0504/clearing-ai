## Thread (8 tweets)

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Before AI, hitting deep work was hard but automatic.

You'd start a tricky problem. 20 minutes in, you'd stop noticing time. Your fingers would slow. The code would start to flow.

That's gone for most engineers now. And nobody talks about why.

---

**Tweet 2 (The mechanism):**
Csikszentmihalyi spent decades studying flow — that state of effortless, autonomous performance.

The prerequisite: uninterrupted time. 50+ minutes minimum before flow kicks in.

The problem: AI prompts are interruptive by design. Each one resets your clock before you ever reach it.

---

**Tweet 3 (The silent casualty):**
The thing that made software engineering feel like a craft — the slow, satisfying descent into a hard problem — is being engineered away.

Not by AI being bad. By AI being too easy to reach.

Ease of access is now inversely correlated with depth of work.

---

**Tweet 4 (The comparison table moment):**
Before AI:
- Hard problem → struggle through it → breakthrough → learning
- The breakthrough WAS the learning

After AI:
- Hard problem → ask AI → get answer → move on
- The ease skips the struggle, and the struggle was where the skill lived

---

**Tweet 5 (What engineers report):**
"The code ships. The PR merges. But I don't feel like I wrote it."

This is the hollow authorship problem. Your output looks like competence. Your internal experience says something different.

That's not burnout. That's a different thing. That's AI fatigue.

---

**Tweet 6 (The flow-killer you don't see):**
Your AI tool has no concept of "I'm almost there."

It responds instantly. Every time you ask, it answers. It never says "try one more time, you're close."

And that instant availability is destroying the conditions flow needs to form.

---

**Tweet 7 (The 90-minute protocol):**
Here's what works:

1. Set a timer for 90 minutes before touching any AI
2. Get stuck. Stay stuck. Work the problem.
3. Only after 90 minutes: open AI for batch queries

The goal isn't to avoid AI. It's to not let AI prevent you from ever reaching the deep work state.
