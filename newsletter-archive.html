The Architecture Problem Nobody Names

The most dangerous AI fatigue isn't for junior engineers. It's for your best senior engineers — the ones who used to raise the hard questions in design review, who noticed the edge cases before they became production incidents. What's happening to them: they're making fewer architecture decisions. Not because AI makes the decisions better. Because AI handles the implementation so the decision feels less urgent. And somewhere in that loop, the practice of deciding slowly atrophies.

Three reasons this hits seniors hardest: the Expertise Reversal Effect (Kalyuga), the Authority Trap (nobody challenges senior decisions anymore, not even AI), and the Junior Visibility Problem (skill atrophy shows up in code reviews; judgment atrophy shows up in production months later).
