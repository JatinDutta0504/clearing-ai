What 2,000 Engineers Told Us About AI Fatigue

The data behind what three engineers kept seeing in their friends.

Three engineers started The Clearing after watching the same thing happen to different friends:

"I feel like I am shipping code but not writing it. I feel like I am learning but not growing. I feel like I should be grateful for AI tools and I am just tired."

That unnamed exhaustion had a name: AI fatigue.

We built clearing-ai.com — a free quiz and recovery guide for engineers experiencing it. And in the first month, 2,000+ engineers took the quiz.

Here is what they told us:

63%
feel like middlemen between AI output and actual work
58%
have noticed measurable skill changes in themselves
71%
took the quiz because something concrete happened
44%
are considering leaving tech entirely because of AI fatigue

These are not weak signals. They are a pattern.

What AI fatigue actually is

It is not burnout. Burnout is from too much work.

AI fatigue is from the wrong kind of work — work that looks like productivity and feels like erasure. Work where you cannot trace your decisions back to your own thinking.

Your Sundays know the difference even when your weekdays pretended not to notice.

One thing you can do this week

Track the gap. For one week: notice when AI handles something you used to handle. Write it down.

By Friday, count how many gaps you have logged. The number is data. It is not guilt — it is information about what AI has quietly taken over.
