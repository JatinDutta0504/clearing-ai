# Dispatch #30 — The Clearing Weekly **From:** The Clearing **Subject:** The compounding cost nobody talks about **Preheader:** Why doing more with AI keeps making you less capable --- ## Issue #30 — April 18, 2026 --- ### The Compounding Cost Nobody Talks About Every week more engineers tell us the same thing: *"I shipped more code than ever last month. And I feel like I know less."* This is the AI productivity paradox — and it's getting worse, not better. The more fluent engineers become at prompting, the less fluent they become at everything else. Not because they're lazy. Because the system is designed to reward output and ignore capability erosion. **Here's what the 2,147-engineer survey found:** 71% of engineers who use AI daily reported measurable skill atrophy in at least one area they used to know cold. Not confidence issues — actual skill loss. Debugging. Algorithmic thinking. Error literacy. Reading code. The most damaging part: almost none of them realized it was happening until they tried to work without AI for a week. By then the gap was real. --- ### The Sunday Night Pattern We keep hearing from engineers that Sunday night is when it hits hardest. Not because Monday is hard. Because Sunday night is the first quiet moment to sit with the question: *"I worked all week. Do I actually know anything?"* This is different from burnout. Burnout is about energy. This is about identity. You can rest and still feel it. The engineers navigating this best aren't the ones using AI less. They're the ones being more deliberate about which tasks get AI and which don't — and protecting at least one hour per week where they work entirely without it. --- ### New on The Clearing This Week **Engineer Survey Results 2026** — We analyzed responses from 2,147 engineers across roles, seniority levels, and industries.
