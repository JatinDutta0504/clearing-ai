# The Dispatch #57 — "The Confirmation Loop"

## Theme
The trap where AI use begets more AI use — how engineers get locked into a cycle that drains their energy and confidence simultaneously.

---

## Structure

**Opening (recognition):**
You've noticed it. Every task starts to feel like it needs AI. Not because you're dependent — because the muscle for doing it yourself has quietly atrophied. So you reach for the shortcut. The shortcut works. You reach faster next time. The loop closes.

**What is the confirmation loop:**
- Step 1: You use AI to solve a problem → it solves it faster than you expected
- Step 2: Your brain registers this as "I couldn't have done it that fast" → subtle competence doubt
- Step 3: Next problem: you reach for AI before even trying → velocity reinforces the pattern
- Step 4: The small attempts stop. The small attempts were where skill lived.
- Step 5: Now every task that doesn't use AI feels slow and uncertain
- Step 6: You're locked in. Not because AI is bad. Because the loop never let you practice.

**The math nobody talks about:**
If you use AI for 60% of your coding tasks, you're practicing 40% of your previous skill. That 40% decays. The 60% grows. But the 40% — the debugging, the architecture decisions, the debugging edge cases — that's where senior engineers live. That's where the work feels like yours.

**What breaking the loop looks like:**
The hardest part isn't the no-AI block. It's the first week of feeling slow. Actually slow. Like you're learning again. That's the point. The discomfort is the signal that you're rebuilding something.

**One engineer, one story:**
"I spent three years using AI for almost everything. When I finally tried to build something without it — a small API for a side project — I couldn't debug a simple 500 error for two days. I used to debug that in twenty minutes. I didn't realize how much I'd lost until I tried to use it."

**This week's challenge:**
One intentional, small, AI-free task. Not a big project. Not a week off. One afternoon where you let yourself be slow on purpose.
