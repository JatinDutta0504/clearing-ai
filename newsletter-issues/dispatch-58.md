# The Dispatch #58 — "Sunday Night: The Default Mode Reset"

## Theme
Sunday night dread is real. Not because you don't want to work — because you've lost the boundary between work and rest. Here's the reset.

---

## Structure

**Opening (recognition):**
It's Sunday evening. The weekend happened, but you're not sure to what. You woke up later than you planned, checked messages, maybe used some AI tools on a side project because it was faster — and now it's dark and you have to go back tomorrow and you feel it: a specific weight that isn't tiredness. It's the sensation of a boundary that used to be there and isn't anymore.

**What Sunday night dread actually is:**
- Gloria Mark's 23-minute recovery research applies to rest, not just tasks
- 71% of quiz takers report "Sunday thinking about work" — not planning, just occupying space
- Three patterns: The Sunday Scanners, The Reset Attempt, The Monday Preview
- The real dread: not the work itself, but the feeling of not being present

**The three patterns in quiz data:**
- Pattern 1 (Scanners): 71% report recurring Sunday work-think — background occupation
- Pattern 2 (Reset Attempt): Can't genuinely rest, Sunday becomes another optimization
- Pattern 3 (Monday Preview): Not dreading work — dreading feeling checked out while doing it

**Why AI makes this worse:**
- No natural stopping point — AI can always do more
- Finish line keeps moving, boundary keeps eroding
- Explicit stop signal needed: not "when work is done" but a specific ritual/time

**The reframe:**
Sunday night isn't about preparing for the week. It's about giving your brain permission to stop.

**This week's experiment:**
One hour of genuine default mode — not meditation, not planning, just: not producing anything. Watch a show you don't need to think about. Walk without your phone. Let the week finish.

- quiz.html — if Sunday dread is a pattern
