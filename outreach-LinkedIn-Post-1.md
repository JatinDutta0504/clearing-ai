---

Something I've been tracking since we launched:

Engineers who've taken our AI Fatigue Quiz score highest not when they're juniors — but when they're 4-8 years in.

That's the sweet spot. Old enough to have real craft pride. Young enough that admitting something's off feels like failure.

The patterns we're seeing:

**They're quieter in code review.** Not because they have nothing to say — because they're not sure what's theirs anymore.

**They stay late not because of workload, but because of context debt.** They can't shake the feeling they should understand their own codebase better than they do.

**They stopped raising edge cases.** Not because they stopped seeing them. Because asking AI to flag them first feels faster than being the person who "should have caught that."
