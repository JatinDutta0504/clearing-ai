# The Offload Loop

A framework for understanding the cognitive dependency spiral created by AI coding tools.

## The Pattern

1. You use AI to think less
2. You practice thinking less
3. Thinking becomes harder
4. You use more AI to compensate
5. It becomes harder to think without AI

## The Self-Reinforcing Loop

The less you practice thinking through hard problems, the slower and more uncomfortable it becomes. The slower it gets, the more you reach for AI to skip the discomfort. The more you skip, the less you practice.

The gap between "can understand AI's answer" and "could have generated it" grows every week.

## The Math

- Before AI: 40 min = 10 min struggle (reps) + 30 min build (output)
- After AI: 5 min prompt + review, then move on

Output doubled. Reps per day dropped 75%. The skill curve looks terrible.

## Breaking the Loop

The engineers who navigate this well aren't the ones using less AI. They're the ones deliberate about which thinking they delegate and which they protect.

Protected thinking = non-negotiable workout. Everything else offloaded.

## The Honesty Check

"Can I explain what the last piece of AI-generated code does — without looking at it?"

- Yes: your mental model is intact
- No: that's not a knowledge gap. That's a dependency gap
