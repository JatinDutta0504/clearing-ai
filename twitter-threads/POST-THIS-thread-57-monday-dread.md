## Tweet 1 (HOOK):
It's Sunday night.

You have nothing concrete to worry about. The PRs are merged. The standup notes are ready. The week looks clear.

You're still dreading it.

If this sounds familiar — you might have AI fatigue, not a motivation problem.

---

## Tweet 2:
Traditional burnout dread came from overwork. You were tired because you worked too much.

AI fatigue dread is different:

You worked normal hours. You shipped a lot. You used the best tools available.

But you spent the entire week processing outputs instead of generating ideas.

The exhaustion is real. The cause is invisible.

---

## Tweet 3:
What "productive" looks like with AI:

→ Answered 84 Slack messages
→ Reviewed 12 AI-generated PRs
→ Attended 9 meetings
→ Processed 3 architecture decisions
→ Shipped 8 features

What you built: a lot
What you learned: almost nothing

---

## Tweet 4:
The Sunday dread has a specific texture for AI-fatigued engineers:

You're not worried about the work being hard.

You're worried about spending another week in the gap — producing without growing, moving without learning, being busy without being challenged.

The work is fine. The work isn't the problem.

---

## Tweet 5:
The thing nobody tells you about AI fatigue:

It's not about using AI too much.

It's about letting AI be the only way you interact with hard problems.

When that becomes your only mode, the dread is your nervous system telling you something important.

---

## Tweet 6:
The diagnostic question:

Are you a better engineer than you were 6 months ago?

Not "are you more productive." Not "do you have more shipped."

Are you better — deeper understanding, wider intuition, stronger pattern recognition — than you were?

If yes, you're probably fine.

If no — that's the gap worth looking at.

---

## Tweet 7:
What the engineers who don't have the Sunday dread do differently:

→ They schedule "no-AI" time not as a restriction, but as a calibration
→ They track what they've learned, not just what they've shipped
→ They notice when their days feel thin — like they're moving through work instead of into it
→ They treat AI as one mode among several, not the default mode for everything

---

## Tweet 8:
The first step isn't a productivity fix.

It's recognizing what's actually happening: you're not unmotivated. You're not lazy. You're in a work mode that generates output without generating growth.

The fix is changing the mode, not pushing harder in the same one.
