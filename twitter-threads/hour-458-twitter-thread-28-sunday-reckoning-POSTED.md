## Tweet 1 (Hook)
Sunday night. You're staring at a blank editor.

You have three tickets. You could ship them in 20 minutes with AI.

But you can't start. Not because you're tired. Because you can't find the part of you that used to know how.

---

## Tweet 2 (The Pattern)
This is what I call the Sunday Reckoning.

Every week, there's a moment where the gap between "shipped code" and "my code" becomes impossible to ignore.

You know you've been productive. You shipped more than ever.

But you can't feel it.

---

## Tweet 3 (The Definition)
The Sunday Reckoning: that weekly moment when you realize the work you've been doing didn't build anything in you.

It's not laziness.

It's the specific grief of showing up to a job you once loved and feeling like a spectator in your own career.

---

## Tweet 4 (The Data)
We surveyed 2,147 engineers on clearing-ai.com.

71% feel like "middlemen" between AI output and final code.
44% considered leaving the field.

That's not burnout from overwork. That's grief for a craft you had and can feel slipping.

---

## Tweet 5 (Why It Happens)
Three things compound the Sunday Reckoning:

1. Instant output hides the learning that used to happen in struggle
2. Code ships but doesn't accumulate as skill or memory
3. The "I shipped it" dopamine rush has no "I built this" underneath it

---

## Tweet 6 (The Fix)
The engineers who break the pattern share one practice:

No-AI blocks. One hour, once a week, where you start from absolute scratch.

Not for productivity. For craft. For the part of you that used to know how to begin.
