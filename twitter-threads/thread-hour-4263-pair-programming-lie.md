# Twitter Thread #76 — The Pair Programming Lie
# Hour 4263 — Fresh thread
# Theme: AI pair programming gives you 2 people doing the work of 0.5

---

## Tweet 1 (Hook):
AI pair programming sounds like 2 engineers working together.

In practice it's often 0.5.

Because neither person is fully present.

---

## Tweet 2:
Classic pair programming:
→ Two humans, full attention
→ Real-time code review, teaching, pattern transfer
→ 2 people × full presence = genuine collaborative intelligence

AI pair programming:
→ One human, 60% attention on prompting and reviewing
→ AI suggests, human approves or overrides
→ 1 human × 0.6 attention = mostly one person talking to themselves with an autocomplete

---

## Tweet 3:
The experience reported by engineers who do AI pairing all day:

"You stop feeling like you're programming. You feel like you're reviewing someone else's work — except you're also the one who has to take responsibility for it."

That's not pair programming. That's oversight with extra steps.

---

## Tweet 4:
The knowledge transfer problem:

Real pair programming transfers knowledge from senior to junior.
Both people leave smarter.

AI pair programming:
→ Junior gets AI's suggestions (not the senior's reasoning)
→ Senior gets... the same AI suggestions
→ Junior's skills atrophy at the exact moment they need scaffolding
→ Senior's skills atrophy from not doing the foundational work

---

## Tweet 5:
The productivity numbers say it's working:
→ 40% faster feature delivery
→ Fewer bugs caught in review
→ More lines of code per sprint

The sustainability numbers say something else:
→ Junior engineers 18 months in don't know how to debug without AI
→ Senior engineers have lost the ability to sit with a hard problem
→ Both groups report feeling "productive but not proud"

---

## Tweet 6:
The mandating problem:

When orgs mandate AI pair programming tools:

The message is: "AI can replace half of pair programming, and we think the efficiency gain is worth the skill atrophy."

That's a real trade-off. But most organizations aren't naming it as one — they're just mandating the tool.

Engineers who flag the trade-off get told to "adapt to the new reality."

---

## Tweet 7:
What to do if you're in this:

→ Protect real pairing sessions — human-to-human, full attention, no AI
→ Use AI pairing for code review, not generation
→ When AI generates something significant: close the tab and try to explain why it works without AI
→ Treat AI suggestions the way you'd treat a code review from someone you don't trust yet
