## Tweet 2 (Sign 1):
**Sign 1: You reason your way to decisions you used to just know.**

Before AI tools, experienced engineers developed a calibrated gut — "this will break in production," "this approach will cause pain in 6 months."

You felt it. Not reasoned it.

Now: you weigh tradeoffs on paper and still miss the obvious problem.

Your gut is still there. It's just being overridden by AI-generated confidence.

---

## Tweet 3 (Sign 2):
**Sign 2: You can explain past decisions but not current ones.**

There's a difference between "understanding why code exists" and "having the judgment to decide what should exist."

Senior ICs used to build judgment over years of shipping, breaking, and fixing.

Now: you ship faster, but the trail of *why* is thinner. You remember fewer lessons because AI absorbed them for you.

---

## Tweet 4 (Sign 3):
**Sign 3: Your feedback loop is inverted.**

Good engineers used to get better by watching their decisions play out — seeing what broke, what scaled, what caused incidents.

Now: AI smooths the feedback. Code ships, tests pass, no visible cost.

But the cost is real. It just moved somewhere you can't see: into your judgment account.
