# Twitter Thread #47 — The Senior Engineer Paradox
# Hour 589 — Fresh thread for Reddit support week
# Theme: Senior engineers feel the most uncertain — not because they're falling behind, but because AI breaks the seniority signals that used to work

---

## Tweet 1 (Hook):
The senior engineers I know are some of the most uncertain people in tech right now.

Not because they're getting worse at their jobs.

Because AI broke the signals they used to trust.

---

## Tweet 2:
When you're a junior dev, uncertainty feels normal. You don't know things. That's expected.

When you're a senior dev with 10 years of pattern recognition, the moment you can't trust your own instincts is different.

It's not "I don't know this."

It's "I used to know this and I can't feel it anymore."

---

## Tweet 3:
Here's what happened:

Seniority was built on: pattern recognition → fast judgment → clean decisions → earned trust.

AI started handling the pattern recognition part.

Now senior engineers are making fast judgments — but they're often AI-assisted judgments, not instinct-backed ones.

The confidence stayed. The foundation shifted.

---

## Tweet 4:
The paradox:

You look MORE competent because AI handles the hard parts.
You feel LESS confident because you can't feel the growth anymore.

These two things are happening at the same time. Nobody talks about how disorienting that is.

---

## Tweet 5:
What I'm hearing from senior engineers:

"I ship faster than ever. I also feel like I'm watching myself work."

"I can debug anything. I can't start anything from scratch anymore."

"My code review looks great. I couldn't explain half of it to you."

---

## Tweet 6:
The dangerous part:

Because seniority used to mean "you know more and faster" — when that changes, your identity as a senior engineer changes too.

It's not just a skill question. It's a "who am I?" question.

And that one is harder to answer.
