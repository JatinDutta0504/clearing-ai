# Twitter Thread #50 — The Estimation Paradox
# Hour 626 — Fresh thread
# Theme: AI makes engineers estimate lower — but that doesn't mean they're actually getting faster

---

## Tweet 1 (Hook):
You used to estimate features at 3 days.

Now you estimate them at 1 day.

Your team is thrilled.

But here's what's actually happening: you're not 3x faster. You're 3x more willing to let AI carry the hard parts.

---

## Tweet 2:
The dangerous part:

When estimates drop dramatically, no one asks why.

Your manager sees: "wow, they're shipping faster!"
Your CTO sees: "AI is working!"
You see: ... (you're not sure what you see)

---

## Tweet 3:
The real question no one is asking:

Did the complexity of the work go down?
Or did you just offload the thinking to a model that doesn't charge by the hour?

---

## Tweet 4:
Here's the hidden cost:

That 3-day estimate included cognitive work:
→ Understanding the system deeply
→ Finding the right approach
→ Catching edge cases in advance
→ Building the mental model

AI skips most of that. You ship. But you didn't do the work of understanding.

---

## Tweet 5:
What this compounds into:

Sprint 1: You estimate 1 day. Ship in 1 day. Great!
Sprint 2: You estimate 1 day. Ship in 1 day. Still good!
Sprint 3: Something breaks. You can't debug it. You don't know the system well enough.
Sprint 4: You ask AI to debug it. It suggests changes. You don't fully understand them.
Sprint 5: The codebase is now a mystery. You are a prompt engineer with an IDE.

---

## Tweet 6:
The velocity metrics look amazing.

The professional competence of the team is quietly depreciating.

This is the estimation paradox: AI makes your estimates accurate in the short term and deeply inaccurate in the long term.
