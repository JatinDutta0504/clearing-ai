# Twitter Thread #60 — The Estimation Paradox

**Theme:** AI makes estimation harder, not easier  
**Hook:** Why your estimates are worse now than before AI  
**Best posting time:** Fri May 15, 9am PST  

---

## TWEET 1 (Hook)

Why do engineers estimate WORSE now that they have AI assistants?

You'd think: AI writes code faster → estimates should drop.

The opposite is happening.

Here's the paradox nobody talks about 🧵

---

## TWEET 2 (The Problem)

Pre-AI estimation: "This will take 3 days"
→ You knew the codebase
→ You knew your own speed
→ Uncertainty was bounded

Post-AI estimation: "This will take 3 days"
→ AI might generate 300 lines in 20 minutes
→ But the review/understand/debug cycle takes 2 days
→ The 20-minute generation becomes a 3-day correction

---

## TWEET 3 (Why It Broke)

AI compressed the WRITING phase
But the UNDERSTANDING phase is now LARGER

You used to spend 80% writing, 20% reviewing.
Now you spend 20% reviewing AI output, 80% understanding if it's correct.

Same total time. Worse confidence.

---

## TWEET 4 (The New Variables)

Your old estimation model had 3 variables:
- Task complexity
- Your skill
- Known unknowns

AI adds 4 new variables:
- How wrong will the AI be?
- How long to spot the errors?
- How long to understand what it wrote vs what you'd have written?
- How much context do you lose by not writing it yourself?

---

## TWEET 5 (Second-Order Effect)

When you don't write the code, you can't calibrate against reality.

Your internal model of "how long does X take" starts drifting.

6 months of AI-assisted work → your estimates are now systematically wrong in a new direction.

You're not overestimating. You're misestimating in a way you can't see.

---

## TWEET 6 (The Session Problem)

Old estimation: plan your day in the morning
New estimation: you need to estimate the SESSION

"Will this AI session take 2 hours or 2 days?"

It depends on:
- How close the AI is to the right answer
- How many edge cases exist
- How deep the rabbit hole goes

These are unknowable until you're in it.

---

## TWEET 7 (Practical Framework)

What actually works:

1. **Scope the AI interaction itself** — "I'll ask 3 questions and evaluate before committing"
2. **Time-box the exploration** — "20 minutes to assess AI's direction, then decide"
3. **Add a correction buffer** — "AI estimate × 2.5" (and adjust based on how wrong you were last time)
4. **Track your accuracy** — keep a simple log of estimated vs actual for AI-assisted tasks

---

## TWEET 8 (The Uncomfortable Truth)

The engineers who estimate BEST in the AI era are the ones who still write code from scratch regularly.

Not because AI is bad.
Because estimation requires calibration.
Calibration requires feedback loops.
Feedback loops require direct experience.

AI removes the direct experience. Your estimates drift until you recalibrate.

---

## TWEET 9 (CTA)

If your estimates have gotten worse since adding AI to your workflow, you're not bad at estimating.

You're experiencing a calibration problem.

The path back: periodic no-AI sessions to rebuild your internal model.
