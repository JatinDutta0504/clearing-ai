# Twitter Thread #69 — The Middleman Problem
# Hour F4509cba — Fresh thread (May 5, 2026, 1:59 AM PDT)
# Theme: AI has become the middleman between engineers and their craft — every prompt is a small act of outsourcing your mind

---

## TWEET 1 (Hook):
There's a middleman between you and your code now.

You might not have invited him. But he's there every time you open the chat.

---

## TWEET 2 (The Setup):
Before AI: you → problem → code → understanding

After AI: you → problem → prompt → AI → code → understanding (sometimes)

The AI inserted itself in the loop.

And here's the thing about middlemen: they take a cut. The middleman in this case takes the struggle. The aha moment. The "I finally get it." The thing that made you a developer in the first place.

---

## TWEET 3 (The Real Cost):
We talk about AI helping us ship faster.

But what did we used to ship?

We shipped understanding. We shipped confidence. We shipped the feeling of "I built this and I know why it works."

Now we're shipping features while slowly losing the thing we actually built — our expertise.

---

## TWEET 4 (The Invisible Trade):
Every time you prompt AI instead of thinking:

- You trade a moment of productive struggle for faster output
- You trade uncertainty for plausible-sounding certainty
- You trade ownership for access

The output arrives. The expertise doesn't.

---

## TWEET 5 (The Craftsman's View):
A woodworker doesn't hand their project to a robot and call it theirs.

A writer doesn't dictate to an AI and call themselves the author.

But as engineers, we've decided the rules are different. Ship the code. Who cares how it got made.

We're losing the craft in the output.

---

## TWEET 6 (The Structural Problem):
The incentives are perfectly designed to make this worse:

- Your manager wants features. AI delivers features.
- Your performance review measures shipped work.
- Your company wants billable hours. AI makes you more billable.
- Nobody measures "depth of understanding" in your sprint review.

The middleman is built into the system.

---

## TWEET 7 (What to Do About It):
The fix isn't rejecting AI.

It's being honest about when the middleman is helping and when he's hurting.

Ask before every prompt:
- Am I using AI to think, or to avoid thinking?
- Will I understand this code in 6 months?
- Is the struggle part of what I need to learn?

If you can't answer yes to the second question, the middleman might be costing more than he's delivering.

---

## TWEET 8 (The Closing):
Every craft has a relationship with its tools.

Carpenters have hand tools and power tools. Writers have pens and word processors.

The tool isn't the problem. The middleman who replaces the relationship is.

You still write the code. You still architect the system. You still own the decisions.

But first, before the prompt — think.

That's the part AI can't do for you.
